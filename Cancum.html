History

A DESERT ISLAND Just four decades ago, Cancun was a desert island and few knew of its existence. Located in the most abandoned region of the Caribbean, it consisted of a dune in the shape of seven, with some sections barely 20 m wide, separated from the mainland by two narrow channels that connected the sea with a wide system of lagoons.
The riverbank was made up of swampy swamps and generally surrounded by mangroves, virgin jungle and unexplored beaches. Its exact name even offered doubts. On some maps the site name appears as "Kankun" (a single word with the letter "k"), which in Mayan means "snake pot" or "snake nest". But in the first minutes of Infratur (before becoming Fonatur), the designation appears separated into two words: Kan Kun and, sometimes, Can Cún (perhaps already Castilianized). Cancun, the current name, is perhaps due to phonetic comforts that obey custom ... or chance.

Xcaret


Xcaret, formerly Polé, was an important commercial city of the Mayan culture before the arrival of the Spanish conquerors in the 16th century. It is currently an ecological theme park located in the Riviera Maya, 5 km south of Playa del Carmen and 75 km south of Cancún, in the state of Quintana Roo, Mexico. For the Mayan civilization this site was an important port and commercial center and where archaeological remains can still be seen.

Museo Subacuático de Arte


It is the third most important museum work carried out by the National Institute of Anthropology and History, behind the National Museum of Anthropology (1964) and the Museo del Templo Mayor (1987). It houses one of the most significant archaeological collections of Mayan culture in the country, since they include not only the most relevant pieces from the State of Quintana Roo, but a selection of emblematic pieces from the sites of Palenque, Chichén Itzá, and Comalcalco, among other sites. represented. The current headquarters was opened in 2012. The space works from Tuesday to Sunday from 09:00 to 18:00. The last entry is at 5:30 p.m. The entry cost for adults is $ 70 pesos; children under 13 are free. It is recommended to wear comfortable shoes, not to introduce food or drinks and not to smoke. This museum has the services of identity cards in Spanish and English, store-bookstore, cafeteria, educational services, parcels and nursing; There is also accessibility for people with special abilities. It is located on Blvd. Kukulkán km 16.5, in the Hotel Zone, Cancun.

én Itzá


Chichen Itza is a world famous Mayan ruins complex in Mexico's Yucatan peninsula. An enormous step pyramid, known as El Castillo, dominates the 6.5 square km of the ancient city, which flourished from about AD 600. until the 13th century. Carved stone graphics survive on structures such as the ball court, Temple of Warriors, and Wall of Skulls. At night, light and sound shows illuminate the sophisticated geometry of the buildings.

Laguna Nichupte


The Nichupté Lagoon is a fascinating natural area with an extension of more than 3,000 hectares in which there are mangroves and seven beautiful lagoons, among which are: Bojórquez, Ciega, San José and Del Amor Lagoons, the latter named for its peculiar heart shape. This almost virgin area is connected to the Caribbean Sea through the Nizuc and Siegfried channels, it inhabits a fabulous plant and animal diversity that make it one of the best natural paradises in Cancun that is worth enjoying.
